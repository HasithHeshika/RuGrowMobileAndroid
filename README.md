🌱 RuGrow – Smart Automated Pepper Cultivation System

RuGrow is an innovative IoT-based smart farming system designed to automate the complete lifecycle of a pepper plant — from growth to harvesting and packaging. Developed by undergraduate students from the Faculty of Engineering, University of Ruhuna, this project will be showcased at Rextro 2025, held on December 5–7, 2025.

The primary goal of RuGrow is to revolutionize small-scale and greenhouse farming by integrating automation, artificial intelligence, and IoT technologies to enhance productivity and sustainability. The system provides an artificially designed transparent environment that allows a pepper plant to grow under precisely controlled conditions.

A smart LED lighting panel simulates sunlight and automatically adjusts the light wavelength and intensity based on the plant’s age and growth stage. This ensures the plant receives the most suitable light colour spectrum for optimal photosynthesis at each phase of its development.

The system continuously monitors soil moisture, temperature, and humidity using IoT sensors, which send real-time data to a central microcontroller. Based on these readings, automated adjustments are made to maintain ideal growth conditions without human intervention.

Once the plant reaches the fruiting stage, image processing and sensor data are used to detect the ripeness and readiness of the peppers. When the system identifies a mature fruit, a robotic arm precisely cuts it from the plant. The harvested fruit is then transferred through an automated tray mechanism, dropped into a packaging unit, and sealed for marketing — completing the entire process seamlessly.

RuGrow demonstrates how IoT, automation, and robotics can be combined to create a fully autonomous agricultural solution. It represents a step toward the future of smart farming, where technology enhances crop yield, reduces manual labour, and promotes sustainable agricultural practices.

Project Type: IoT | Automation | Smart Agriculture | Robotics
Exhibition: Rextro 2025 – Faculty of Engineering, University of Ruhuna
